  - Inspire participation in Hackathon competitions to turn theory into real products under time pressure.
  - Raise awareness of systems thinking and DevOps foundations to thoroughly solve hidden project issues.

- **Khac Uy Pham:** Topic "The Iceberg of Procrastination".

## 3. Session Highlights

### Effective AWS Hands-On Learning (Speaker: Huynh Thai Linh)

- **Barrier:** The biggest challenge for AWS beginners is the fear of bills, forgetting to delete resources, and unexpected costs.
- **Solution:** Use AWS Cloud Quest (game-based learning) and Floci (free local AWS emulator, instant startup in 24ms, supporting 50+ services) to practice safely without cloud costs.

### Overcoming the Procrastination Iceberg (Speaker: Khac Uy Pham)

- **Nature of procrastination:** Laziness is only 20-30% of the surface. The submerged 70-80% actually stems from difficulty regulating emotions and deep-seated fears (fear of not being good enough, fear of judgment, fear of failure).
- **Consequence:** Avoidance creates "The Guilt Loop," increasing stress and worsening procrastination.
- **Action:** Apply the "5-minute rule" to start taking action immediately, because action itself creates confidence.

### The Importance of Confidence (Speaker: Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu)

- **New definition:** Confidence is not about having a big ego or knowing everything, but daring to try even when anxious. Technical skills are just the foundation; confidence is the bridge connecting communication, collaboration, and idea advocacy.
- **Risk:** Lack of confidence, Imposter Syndrome, or the Dunning-Kruger effect can cause us to miss opportunities and bury our potential.

### Hackathon Hands-On Experience (The Ballers Team)

- **Context:** Hackathons are fast-paced playgrounds (24-48h) focused on creating prototypes or MVPs to solve real-world problems.
- **Core value:** Learning by doing (2 days of practice is more effective than weeks of theory), expanding networks (meeting developers, recruiters), and unleashing creativity.
- **Real story:** The team shared their challenging 36-hour journey (sleep deprivation, laptop RAM failure, fast food meals) to successfully build two projects: SynthHunter (AI sound classification system for human/machine audio) and Vortex (comprehensive recruitment process). Outstanding result: both projects made it to the Top 10 AWS Track.

### The Hidden Iceberg of Projects & DevOps Mindset (Speaker: Tran Minh Quan)

- **Systems Thinking:** Visible problems (missed deadlines, production bugs, team burnout) are only the tip of the iceberg. The real submerged part comes from vague requirements, poor communication, manual processes, and slow feedback loops. Engineers need to find root causes instead of firefighting.
- **DevOps essence:** DevOps is not just about tools (Docker, CI/CD) but the combination of People, Process, and Technology. You can automate code deployment, but you cannot automate trust or communication.
- **4 Core Pillars of DevOps:**
  - Collaboration: Breaking down silos between teams.
  - Automation: Minimizing human error.
  - Fast Feedback: Monitoring and automated testing.
  - Continuous Improvement: Learning from every failure.

## 4. Value & Lessons Learned

### Technical & Process Mindset

- **Testing architecture:** Use Floci to test AWS services locally before deploying to real AWS to eliminate cost risks.
- **DevOps Mindset:** CI/CD tools only work effectively when a team culture of shared responsibility and barrier removal is firmly established.
- **Product development:** Always start from a real user "pain point," continuously experiment, and maximize available tools to optimize progress.

### Self-Development Mindset

- **Action drives confidence:** Don't wait for perfection. Instead of blaming laziness, name your fears and take small steps to overcome procrastination.
- **Learn by doing:** Throwing yourself into high-intensity challenges like Hackathons provides hands-on experience far beyond theoretical learning.
- **Systems thinking:** Look at every problem (both personal and project-related) deeper than the surface to solve root causes.

## 5. Application Plan for Work & Study

- **AWS practice:** Install Floci for personal exercises to safely test S3, Lambda, and DynamoDB on local machine.
- **Try Hackathons:** Plan to find teammates and register for a Hackathon this year to practice MVP development skills under time pressure.
- **Team improvement:** Apply DevOps lessons to academic projects: build a blame-free culture, enhance communication, and set up basic automation testing/CI.
- **Self-management:** Apply the "5-4-3-2-1" countdown technique when facing speaking opportunities to build confidence, and use the "5-minute rule" to force yourself to open the laptop and code whenever procrastination hits.

## 6. Personal Experience & Lessons

- **Emotional experience:** The event brought multi-dimensional emotions: from practical technology methods, deep psychological analysis, to the passionate, "crazy," and explosive energy of youth through the 36-hour non-stop coding story of the Hackathon team. I realized that the daily minor mistakes and missed deadlines in my project team were actually the "submerged iceberg" related to communication and manual processes. The event ignited my spirit, helping me understand that even talented speakers and award-winning teams all started from fear and rose through countless mistakes.
- **Confidence and procrastination:** Confidence is not innate; it is built through thorough preparation and the courage to act despite fear. Similarly, procrastination is only broken when we dare to take the first step.
- **Real-world immersion:** Continuously put yourself in real environments (like Hackathons) to gain experience, learn perseverance, and develop flexible problem-solving skills.
- **Human factor:** No matter how advanced the technology (AWS, Docker, CI/CD), people, communication, and systems thinking remain the decisive factors in the success or failure of every project.
