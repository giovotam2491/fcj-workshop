During my internship, I participated in three events. Each one was a memorable experience that provided new, interesting, and useful knowledge, along with gifts and wonderful moments.

### [Event 1](4.1-Event1/)
&emsp;**Event Name:** FCAJ Community Day

&emsp;**Date and Time:** 09:00 - 12:00, May 23, 2026

&emsp;**Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

&emsp;**Your Role in the Event:** Attendee

&emsp;**Description:** A networking event for the tech community, focusing on sharing in-depth knowledge about cloud computing and artificial intelligence. Industry experts presented techniques for optimizing AI context, applications of the Amazon Q virtual assistant, CloudFront network infrastructure solutions, the uncertainty of large language models (LLMs), enterprise-grade multi-agent system architecture, and practical experience from a fast-paced coding competition (hackathon).

&emsp;**Outcomes and Value Gained:**
- Learned the practical process of deploying AI onto the AWS cloud platform. Gained a clear understanding of the probabilistic nature of AI (how to adjust randomness parameters) and formed an enterprise-grade software development mindset, where systems must ensure security and data compliance right from the initial design phase.
- Eliminated common misconceptions regarding AI configuration and the risks of relying on a single AI agent. Was inspired by sprint projects under high time pressure, which helped clearly define my career orientation: to cultivate additional skills in security and system deployment rather than focusing solely on writing code.


### [Event 2](4.2-Event2/)
&emsp;**Event Name:** Saturday Meet up

&emsp;**Date and Time:** 09:00 - 12:00, May 30, 2026

&emsp;**Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

&emsp;**Your Role in the Event:** Attendee

&emsp;**Description:** The event focused on sharing safe and cost-optimized practical methods for learning the AWS cloud platform (via Cloud Quest and Floci), while also addressing psychological challenges such as how to overcome procrastination and build confidence. Additionally, the program provided inspiration through a 36-hour real-world hackathon story and offered an in-depth analysis of systems thinking and DevOps culture in project development.

&emsp;**Outcomes and Value Gained:**
- Learned how to use the Floci tool to simulate AWS on a personal computer, enabling safe and cost-free testing. Deeply understood that the core of DevOps is the combination of people, process, and technology—not just CI/CD tools. Acquired practical psychological techniques, such as the "5-minute rule," to break the cycle of procrastination.
- Realized that visible issues like missed deadlines or project bugs often stem from a "hidden iceberg" of poor communication and manual processes. Received strong motivation to participate in hackathons to practice building real-world products under high pressure, and learned how to apply systems thinking to solve the root causes of problems in both work and study.
