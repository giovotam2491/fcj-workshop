During my internship, I participated in two events. Each one was a memorable experience that provided new, interesting, and useful knowledge, along with gifts and wonderful moments.

---

### **Event 1**

* **Event Name:** FCAJ Community Day

* **Date & Time:** 09:00 - 12:00, May 23, 2026
* **Location:** 36th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam
* **Role:** Attendee

* **Description:** A technology community event focused on sharing in-depth knowledge about cloud computing and artificial intelligence through 6 discussion sessions. Industry experts presented on AI context optimization techniques (AI Mindset), Amazon Q virtual assistant applications, networking infrastructure solutions and CloudFront's Flat-rate pricing mechanism, the uncertainty of large language models, enterprise-grade multi-agent system architecture, and insights from hackathon competition teams.

* **Outcomes & Value Gained:**
  * Understood the practical AI deployment process on AWS cloud. Grasped the probabilistic nature of AI (how to adjust randomness parameters) and developed enterprise-grade software engineering mindset where systems must ensure security and data compliance from the initial design phase.
  * Eliminated misconceptions about AI configuration and risks of using single AI agents. Gained inspiration from high-pressure sprint projects, and clearly identified career direction — needing to strengthen skills in security and system deployment rather than focusing solely on coding.

### **Event 2**

* **Event Name:** Saturday Meetup (FCAJ Challenger Hackathon Side Event)

* **Date & Time:** 09:00 - 12:00, May 30, 2026
* **Location:** 36th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam
* **Role:** Attendee

* **Description:** The event focused on sharing safe and cost-optimized AWS cloud practices (using Cloud Quest and Floci tools), while addressing psychological challenges such as overcoming procrastination and building self-confidence. Attendees also heard the intense 36-hour sprint stories from Hackathon teams (with notable projects like AI SynthHunter voice analytics solution and Vortex recruitment system), combined with expert analyses on systems thinking and DevOps culture.

* **Outcomes & Value Gained:**
  * Learned how to use Floci to simulate AWS on a personal machine for safe, cost-free testing. Understood that the core of DevOps is the combination of people, process, and technology — not just CI/CD tools. Acquired practical psychological methods like the "5-minute rule" to break procrastination loops.
  * Recognized that issues like schedule delays or project bugs often stem from the "iceberg" of communication gaps and manual processes.
  * Gained strong motivation from the participating teams (e.g., "The Ballers" team that made it to the Top 10 AWS Track for both projects), reinforcing determination to join future Hackathons to develop real product-thinking under high pressure, while learning to apply systems thinking to solve root-cause problems in work and study.
